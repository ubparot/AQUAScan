19  Industry Support and Real-World Relevance  AquaScan also gained real-world relevance through collaboration with MI Technical Solutions, a maritime-focused engineering and technology company whose work relates to technical systems and design in the naval and maritime field. Because AquaScan was designed as a water quality monitoring vessel, we saw a strong connection between our project and the kind of real-world engineering context their company works in.  After reaching out and developing that connection, our team held multiple discussions with the company and prepared a formal pitch explaining the problem, our solution, and the resources needed to fully develop the prototype. Throughout that process, MI Technical Solutions also provided mentorship by helping us refine both our presentation of AquaScan and the way we communicated its technical value. That pitch was ultimately presented to the company’s CEO, Mike Ihrig, along with other members of the company, which gave AquaScan the opportunity to be reviewed in a professional setting rather than only as a competition idea. As part of that process, we requested $1,600 in project funding to support full prototype development, and that support is currently being finalized. The company also expressed strong interest in the project itself, including continued mentorship and the possibility of extending the relationship beyond TSA as AquaScan develops further.  This involvement is important because it shows that AquaScan was strong enough as a concept to gain outside interest from a company whose work already relates to maritime engineering. It also reflects the initiative required to move the project beyond competition requirements and into a more realistic development process. Rather than existing only as a student prototype, AquaScan was presented, reviewed, and supported in a context that reflects broader technical value and real-world application.
