8  CHOSEN SOLUTION: AquaScan (Combined Solution)  Overview:  AquaScan is a solution using the strongest advantages of our above 3 solutions combined into one. This solution uses a small semi-autonomous surface vessel along with a deployable multi-sensor probe system designed to help researchers and environmental organizations monitor water quality more effectively. It combines direct in-water sensing, GPS-based positioning, controlled depth sampling, and digital data output to improve how aquatic conditions are measured and interpreted. By combining mobility with below-surface measurement, AquaScan can collect more detailed and useful environmental data than many traditional monitoring methods that rely only on fixed stations, surface readings, or manual sampling.  The system integrates a dual-pontoon vessel design that supports the propulsion system, electronics, battery, and sensor deployment mechanism in one stable floating platform. The vessel travels to selected sampling points, stops, and lowers its probe beneath the surface to collect readings at controlled depths. This allows AquaScan to gather information through the water column instead of only measuring conditions near the surface. The deployable probe carries the sensor package, while the vessel provides movement, power, positioning, and communication back to the operator interface.  AquaScan addresses the theme   “Engineering the Tools of Scientific Discovery”   by improving how aquatic data is collected and used in water-quality research. Many current monitoring methods can collect useful information, but they are often limited by surface-only readings, fixed sampling locations, delayed manual collection, or data that is difficult to compare across depth and location. AquaScan improves on these limitations by connecting direct sensor measurements with GPS position and probe depth, giving researchers a clearer way to study changes within a body of water. This makes the system more useful for identifying environmental stress, comparing sampling points, and supporting decisions in aquatic research and restoration.  In summary, AquaScan combats this problem by integrating direct sensing, controlled depth monitoring, GPS-based sampling, and digital visualization into one practical research platform. The system not only improves how water-quality data is collected, but also makes that information easier to interpret and apply. By using AquaScan, researchers can gather stronger scientific information, support better environmental decision-making, and improve the protection and understanding of aquatic ecosystems.
