10  Electronics and Power System  AquaScan’s electronics are centered in the sealed electronics tray mounted in the middle of the boat, which serves as the main hub for onboard power and control. The system is powered by a 12-volt, 100-amp-hour lithium battery, which supplies energy to both the boat and the probe and gives AquaScan an expected runtime of about 30-40 minutes. This central battery feeds the main onboard electronics, while fuses help protect the system and keep power routed to the correct components. The Arduino Mega acts as the main brain of the vessel, managing the overall system, while a surface ESP32 serves as the communication link between the operator interface and the onboard control system by receiving commands and passing that information to the Mega.  Power from the battery is also sent directly to the electronic speed controllers (ESCs), which regulate output to the two propulsion motors. In the probe, a second ESP32 acts as the local controller for the sensor package, reading the environmental sensors and sending that information back to the boat through the marine cable tether, which carries both power and data between the vessel and the probe. This allows the probe to function as a depth-based sensing unit while the boat-side ESP32 and Arduino Mega receive, organize, and relay the data back to the operator. Overall, this system gives AquaScan one central power source and one coordinated electronics platform for propulsion, probe communication, and live data collection.  Propulsion and Cooling System  Motor and Drive Layout:  Each pontoon contains one 1100 kv brushless motor mounted inside the hull. These motors drive external propellers through rotating shafts, allowing AquaScan to generate thrust in the water while keeping the motors protected inside the vessel. This setup was selected because it is better suited for a low-speed, high-payload platform rather than a more speed-oriented propulsion setup. It also made the system easier to waterproof and integrate into the printed hull structure. AquaScan uses CNC metal propellers selected for high thrust efficiency and durability, allowing the vessel to move a heavy load more effectively while maintaining more consistent performance under sustained operation.  Shaft Sealing and Power Transfer:  Because the motors are not directly submerged, each drive shaft passes through a grease-lubricated stuffing tube.
