Autonomous Research Vessel for Depth-Aware Water-Quality Monitoring


===== PAGE 2 =====
The Problem  Water-quality monitoring often lacks the depth and location context needed to represent real conditions  Surface-Only Data   Limited Field Coverage   Difficult Data Interpretation  Misses subsurface changes in oxygen, clarity, temperature, and light  Fixed or manual methods can miss site variation and be difficult in shallow areas  Raw readings are harder to compare without location, depth, or visual context


===== PAGE 3 =====
Why This Maers  Scientists need complete, organized data to understand how water conditions actually change  Environmental Conditions  Better data helps scientists identify changes that cannot be judged from the surface alone  Targeted Restoration  Location- and depth-linked readings help show where follow-up testing or restoration should be focused  Clearer Communication  Maps, graphs, heat maps, and depth profiles make results easier to compare and explain

===== PAGE 11 =====
Propulsion & Motor System  Shaft & stuffing tube  Rotating shafts pass through sealed tubes to transfer power to external propellers while preventing water entry.  Internal motor layout  Brushless motors are mounted inside the hull for protection and reliability.  Passive water cooling  Water flows through motor jackets during motion to reduce heat buildup.
